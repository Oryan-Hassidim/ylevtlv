A New World of Encounter

In the yeshiva, we create a space for encounter. A real meeting of souls, a meeting of each of us with the Torah, and a meeting place for the Torah with Israeli reality and the Tel Aviv story.

And perhaps most of all - a meeting of each one with oneself. Without filters. Who am I really and what do I really want to do? What do I truly believe in? What aspects of myself do I want to develop?

The yeshiva is our meeting place with all sorts of people from diverse backgrounds.

A New World of Trust

Everything to us is based on trust, and first and foremost - trust in ourselves. We are good, holy, and sweet people, and our desires are in a good direction and have real meaning.

Our understandings of Torah - surely come from Hashem and we need to trust them, listen to them, and relate to them even if we do not accomplish them immediately.

And so, towards every member of Am Yisrael - we believe that they too have a letter in the Torah, and if we do not listen and relate to it seriously as part of the Torah and the word of God in the world - the Torah within the people of Israel will be lacking.

And of course - we have complete trust in our Torah and our tradition. We know it contains everything. We believe in our land, our state, our city, and our people.

"אשר נתן לנו תורת אמת וחיי עולם נטע בתוכנו"

Oct. 7

The evening preceding the monstrous assault on the people of Israel, we gathered to dance around the Torah amidst the vibrant atmosphere of Tel Aviv. Amidst a year rife with internal strife, we recognized the heightened significance of uniting during the holidays.

The morning dawned with the piercing wail of sirens punctuating the tranquility of Tel Aviv. Oblivious to the conflict raging in the south, students and guests were summoned by the army to prepare and head towards their bases. Slowly, they departed from the prayers until only a handful of elders and non-combatants remained. "I'll ensure the safety of everyone," assured one married student to his tearful 2-year-old son...

Throughout the war, our students valiantly battled on the frontlines, many assuming leadership roles as commanders. Even amidst the depths of Gaza, they endeavored to inspire hope and spread joy among their comrades.

Rav Nir Yaakov Mass, the head of the Yeshiva, aged 50 and far beyond the age of exemption, joined the fray. Utilizing his specialized training, he led convoys behind enemy lines in Khan Yunis. Despite sustaining a head injury during one operation, he made a full recovery and returned to accomplish his missions.

Tragically, we mourned losses close to home as two former students fell in combat: Lavi Gihassi of the Nahal Brigade and Uriel Silverman of the 4th Tanks Reserves Brigade.

May their memories be a blessing.

Sergeant Lavi Ghasi z"l

Sergeant first class (res.) Aviad Silberman z"l

You are invited to participate in strengthening the yeshiva

Everyone can be a partner with us in creating great Torah and Israeli culture!

The Yeshiva "Lev Tel Aviv - Shir L'neriya", named after Neria Cohen, who was tragically killed by terrorists in Jerusalem in 2008, is situated and operates in the heart of Tel Aviv. The yeshiva encompasses the following components:

  1. Heseder Yeshiva: This program combines Torah study with service in the IDF and includes a Kollel Avrechim as well.
  2. Machon Yair: an institution for ba'alei teshuvah.
  3. Community: The yeshiva community resides and actively participates in the city.

You are welcome to donate either one-time or a consistent contribution to join us in supporting the scholars and students.
